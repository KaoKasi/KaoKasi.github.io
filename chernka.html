Define

Following the creation of the user journey and storyboard, I have identified key pain points within the current user flow. These insights are crucial in understanding the challenges users face, paving the way for a more refined and user-centric design in the Define phase.

    Limited Availability:
    • Challenge in finding available seats at popular restaurants.
    Communication Hassles:
    • Difficulty in reaching and contacting the chosen restaurant.
    Time Pressure:
    • Time-consuming process of making calls for reservations.
    • Concerns about the time it takes to get information and confirm details.
    Weather and Queue Discomfort:
    • Displeasure with the inconvenience of waiting in queues, especially in unfavorable weather conditions.
    Public Pressure:
    • Guilt associated with being picky in a public dining setting.
    Anxiety and Overwhelm:
    • Feeling overwhelmed by the number of restaurant choices.
    • Anxiety about making the right menu and table selections.
    Uncertainty:
    • Uncertainty about the availability of desired dishes and tables.
    Menu Selection Concerns:
    • Anxiety about choosing the right items from the menu.
    • Worry about the quality of the menu offerings.
    Order Accuracy:
    • Concern about accurately remembering and communicating the order details.

Test

I initiated by planning usability tests. After two rounds of studies, insights from the initial test informed the progression from wireframes to mockups. The second study, employing a high-fidelity prototype, pinpointed areas in the mockups that required refinement.

1st rounded test results:

Results summary

  • User experienced uncertainty about where to click after selecting the date.
  • Confusion arose due to the calendar icon not explicitly indicating confirmation.
  • Ambiguity surrounding the "looks good" button raised concerns for the user.
  • User found the menu homepage layout confusing.
  • Difficulty in identifying the next step after choosing a table.
  • Struggle to locate the reserve button.
